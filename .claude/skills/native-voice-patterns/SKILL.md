# Native Voice Patterns

A swipe file of hook templates from real scroll-stopping content. These are not invented frameworks. They are structural patterns that already proved they work on actual social feeds.

## How to Use

This is a mid-execution resource. Use it when:
- scroll-stop-hooks is running, you have your trigger and persona
- A hook draft sounds like an ad instead of a person
- You need the sentence to feel native to the platform

Pull a template that matches the emotional register. Fill in the [bracketed variables] with product, persona, or pain-specific language.

## Cluster 1: Confession and Gatekeeping
*"I know something you do not, and I am finally telling you"*

**I have been gatekeeping something that is so niche...**
- Tactic: Exclusivity / Curiosity
- Trigger: Curiosity Gap
- Example: *"I have been gatekeeping a productivity system that is so niche my workflow-obsessed friends do not even know it exists."*

**Someone once told me [X] and I have not been the same since**
- Tactic: Storytelling / Confession
- Trigger: Curiosity Gap, Pain Agitation
- Example: *"Someone once told me that most teams are spending 80% of their Claude usage on things a good workspace would automate, and I have not been the same since."*

**Thank you to everyone who told me that [X]**
- Tactic: Social Proof / Relatability
- Trigger: Social Proof, Curiosity Gap
- Example: *"Thank you to everyone who told me that setting up context files would change my workflow. You were right and I owe you."*

## Cluster 2: Confusion and Discovery
*"Wait, how did I not know this?"*

**So here is what I do not understand**
- Tactic: Contrarian / Question
- Trigger: Curiosity Gap, Pattern Interrupt
- Example: *"So here is what I do not understand. Why are teams still writing meeting summaries by hand when AI can do it in 10 seconds?"*

**Apparently I have been living in the Stone Age because what do you mean...**
- Tactic: Relatability / Shocking Statement
- Trigger: Pattern Interrupt, Curiosity Gap
- Example: *"Apparently I have been living in the Stone Age because what do you mean you can set up a complete AI workspace in 5 minutes?"*

**Who was gonna tell me...**
- Tactic: Relatability / Confession
- Trigger: Pain Agitation, Curiosity Gap
- Example: *"Who was gonna tell me that the reason my AI outputs are generic is not the model, it is my setup? No one. I had to find out myself."*

## Cluster 3: Hot Takes and Contrarian Angles
*"Everyone believes X but I am about to complicate that"*

**My hot take is that [X], but here is [the thing that contradicts it]**
- Tactic: Contrarian / Belief
- Trigger: Pattern Interrupt, Myth-Busting
- Example: *"My hot take is that most companies do not actually have an AI adoption problem, but here is why that makes their AI adoption problem worse."*

**If you want [X]... [unexpected consequence]**
- Tactic: Contrarian / If-Then
- Trigger: Pattern Interrupt, Curiosity Gap
- Example: *"If you want better AI outputs... stop giving it more instructions."*

**[X] do not want you to know this because then [Y]**
- Tactic: Contrarian / Myth Busting
- Trigger: Myth-Busting, Urgency/Stakes
- Example: *"Enterprise AI vendors do not want you to know this because then you would realize the expensive part is the setup, not the software."*

**I think it is about time we all just said it...**
- Tactic: Contrarian / Belief
- Trigger: Pattern Interrupt, Myth-Busting
- Example: *"I think it is about time we all just said it. Most AI tools are only as good as the person configuring them."*

## Cluster 4: Experience Arc
*"I learned the hard way so you do not have to"*

**The first time I did [X] I did [A]. The second time, I knew better.**
- Tactic: Storytelling / Confession
- Trigger: Social Proof, Pain Agitation
- Example: *"The first time I set up a Claude workspace I wrote everything from scratch. The second time, I knew better than to start from zero."*

**If I had 90 days to [X], here is what I would do**
- Tactic: How To / Authority
- Trigger: Aspiration/Desire, Curiosity Gap
- Example: *"If I had 90 days to get an entire marketing team productive with AI, here is exactly what I would do."*

## Cluster 5: Selective Audience
*"This is specifically for you, and you know who you are"*

**This question is for [hyper-specific qualifier] and only [that qualifier]...**
- Tactic: Demographic Callout / Challenge
- Trigger: Identity Call-Out
- Example: *"This question is for the marketing lead running a team of 5+ and only the marketing lead running a team of 5+. Are you actually using AI or just talking about it?"*

## Cluster 6: Proximity Framing
*"Here is the fastest shortcut to understanding what this is"*

**Make the [X] of [Y]**
- Tactic: Aspirational / Bold Claim
- Trigger: Aspiration/Desire, Pattern Interrupt
- Example: *"Make the Notion of AI workspaces. Opinionated, beautiful, and impossible to go back from."*

## Cluster 7: Soft Signals
*"Low-key, no-pressure, but pay attention"*

**Just a heads up**
- Tactic: Warning / Direct Address
- Trigger: Urgency/Stakes, Pattern Interrupt
- Example: *"Just a heads up. The way most people use ChatGPT is like buying a sports car and never leaving the parking lot."*

**Is there anything better than...**
- Tactic: Aspirational / Relatability
- Trigger: Aspiration/Desire, Identity Call-Out
- Example: *"Is there anything better than opening your laptop Monday morning and your entire week is already planned?"*

## Cluster 8: Cultural Commentary
*"Something in the world just revealed a bigger truth"*

**That is a [X] indicator**
- Tactic: Contrarian / Statistic
- Trigger: Pattern Interrupt, Curiosity Gap
- Example: *"Companies cutting AI budgets right now? That is a productivity indicator. And not the kind they think they are avoiding."*
