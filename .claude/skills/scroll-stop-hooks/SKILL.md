# Scroll-Stop Hooks

Write hooks that stop the scroll, trigger an emotional response, and pull people into the content. A hook's only job is to make someone stop and read (or watch). It does this by triggering a psychological response in the first 1-3 seconds.

## Required Inputs

Before writing, confirm:

1. **Product or topic** — what is it, what does it do
2. **Pain or desire** — what specific problem or aspiration are you targeting
3. **Persona** — who are you talking to, their specific life context
4. **Awareness stage** — where is this audience in their journey (see below)
5. **Format** — video hook (spoken), text hook (static/caption), email subject, landing page headline
6. **Quantity** — how many hooks

If any are missing, ask before writing. Bad inputs produce wasted hooks.

**Related skills:** For a library of 33 tactic types to choose from before writing, use hook-tactics-library. For native social voice templates, use native-voice-patterns.

## The 5 Awareness Stages

The awareness stage determines hook strategy. Wrong stage means the hook misses completely.

| Stage | Who | Hook Strategy |
|-------|-----|---------------|
| **Unaware** | Does not know they have a problem | Introduce the pain through a relatable situation. No product mention. |
| **Problem-Aware** | Knows the problem, no solution | Agitate the pain hard. Make them feel understood. Build urgency. |
| **Solution-Aware** | Comparing options | Differentiate. Position against alternatives. Call out what failed them. |
| **Product-Aware** | Knows your product, has not bought | Remove objections. Trigger FOMO. Social proof. |
| **Most-Aware** | Ready to buy | Direct offer, urgency, guarantee. CTA-forward. |

## The 8 Psychological Triggers

Every strong hook uses at least one. The best combine two.

### 1. Pattern Interrupt
Break mental autopilot. Say something unexpected or counterintuitive.
- "Stop moisturizing your face."
- "I spent $0 on ads last month and got our best results ever."

### 2. Identity Call-Out
Make the right people self-select. Hyper-specific identity triggers higher engagement.
- "If you are a PM who has never touched a terminal, this is for you."
- "This is for the marketer who cancels meetings because the deck is not ready."

### 3. Pain Agitation
Mirror the viewer's internal monologue back at them.
- "You have tried everything and your pipeline is still leaking."
- "The worst part is not the problem itself. It is explaining it to your boss."

### 4. Curiosity Gap
Create an open loop they need to close.
- "Nobody is talking about this change to how Claude handles context."
- "The thing about productivity tools that every SaaS company gets wrong."

### 5. Social Proof / Credibility
Real people, real results, real specificity.
- "We have been doing this for 200 clients. Here is what actually works."
- "She doubled her pipeline in 6 weeks. Here is exactly what she did."

### 6. Contrarian / Myth-Busting
Challenge a belief they hold.
- "Your CRM is the reason your reps are slow."
- "The problem is not your product. It is what your agency told you to do."

### 7. Aspiration / Desire
Show them what is possible.
- "Imagine opening your laptop Monday morning and everything is already organized."
- "You are one workspace away from not thinking about setup anymore."

### 8. Urgency / Stakes
Make inaction feel costly.
- "Every day you do not fix this, your competitors get further ahead."
- "Your launch is in 4 weeks. What are you doing about positioning?"

## Hook Formats

**Video hooks (TikTok, Reels, YouTube):**
- First words spoken. Should work even without visuals.
- Under 10 words for the opening beat. Expand after.
- Match the energy of the platform (casual for TikTok, professional for LinkedIn video).

**Text hooks (static ads, captions, posts):**
- First line must work as a standalone sentence.
- No em dashes, ellipses, or clickbait punctuation unless the platform rewards it.
- LinkedIn: one sentence per line, break aggressively.

**Email subject lines:**
- Under 50 characters. Front-load the trigger.
- Personalization tokens only if they add real specificity.

**Landing page headlines:**
- Benefit-first. State the outcome, not the feature.
- Pair with a subhead that adds specificity or proof.

## Writing Rules

1. One trigger per hook. If combining two, one must dominate.
2. Specificity beats cleverness. "Saved 4 hours per week" beats "save time."
3. Write 3x more hooks than needed. Cut the weakest half.
4. Every hook must pass the "so what?" test. If a stranger would not care, rewrite.
5. Do not start every hook the same way. Vary sentence structure, length, and opening word.
6. Label each hook with its primary trigger so results can be compared.

## Output Format

Group hooks by trigger category. For each hook, note:
- The hook text
- Primary trigger
- Awareness stage it targets
- Format (video / text / email / headline)
