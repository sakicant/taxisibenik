# Influencer Campaign Manager

Run influencer campaigns that produce measurable results. Most influencer marketing fails because of poor selection, vague briefs, or no measurement plan.

## Writes
- `outputs/influencer-campaign-[name].md`

## Step 1: Campaign Strategy

Define before outreach:
1. **Goal** — awareness, traffic, conversions, content creation, or social proof
2. **Audience** — who should see this content (demographics, interests, platforms)
3. **Budget** — total budget and per-influencer range
4. **Platform** — Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, or multi-platform
5. **Timeline** — campaign dates, content deadlines, reporting period
6. **Content type** — sponsored post, story, video, review, unboxing, tutorial

## Step 2: Influencer Selection

**Tier framework:**

| Tier | Followers | Typical Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K-10K | Gift or $50-200 | Niche communities, authentic engagement |
| Micro | 10K-50K | $200-2K | Targeted reach, strong engagement rates |
| Mid | 50K-250K | $2K-10K | Balanced reach and engagement |
| Macro | 250K-1M | $10K-50K | Broad awareness, brand credibility |
| Mega | 1M+ | $50K+ | Mass awareness, celebrity association |

**Selection criteria (weighted):**
- Audience alignment (do their followers match your target?) — 40%
- Engagement rate (likes + comments / followers, not just count) — 25%
- Content quality (would you share their content?) — 20%
- Brand safety (controversial history, competitor affiliations) — 15%

## Step 3: Outreach

**Message structure:**
1. Specific compliment on their content (prove you actually follow them)
2. Who you are and what your product does (one sentence)
3. Why they are a fit (be specific)
4. What you are proposing (content type, compensation, timeline)
5. Next step (call, DM, or email)

## Step 4: Brief

Send to every confirmed influencer:
- Product description and key messages (3-5 talking points, not scripts)
- What to avoid (claims, competitor mentions, off-brand language)
- Required disclosures (#ad, #sponsored, platform-specific rules)
- Content format and length expectations
- Approval process and deadlines
- Usage rights (where you can repost, for how long)
- Payment terms and timeline

## Step 5: Measurement

Track per influencer and overall:
- **Reach** — impressions, views
- **Engagement** — likes, comments, saves, shares
- **Traffic** — link clicks, UTM tracking
- **Conversions** — sales, signups, downloads (use unique codes or links)
- **CPM** — cost per 1,000 impressions
- **CPE** — cost per engagement
- **ROAS** — revenue generated / total spend
