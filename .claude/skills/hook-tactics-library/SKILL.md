# Hook Tactics Library

A strategic reference library. It answers two questions per tactic:
1. What is this tactic? (definition + example)
2. When should I use it? (deployment guidance)

For how to write the hook once a tactic is selected, use scroll-stop-hooks.

## Tactics vs Psychological Triggers

These operate at different levels:
- **Tactics** = the strategic frame or format of the hook (the what)
- **Triggers** = the emotional mechanism that makes it work (the how)

Every tactic is executed through one or more triggers. A Contrarian tactic runs on a Pattern Interrupt trigger. A Demographic Callout runs on Identity Call-Out. When writing hooks by tactic, choose the trigger that best serves the persona and awareness stage.

## The 33 Tactics

### Aspirational
Frames the identity or lifestyle the viewer wants. Speaks to who they want to become.
- **Best for:** Unaware, Problem-Aware. Lifestyle, beauty, fitness, status products.
- **Trigger:** Aspiration/Desire
- *"Glow like never before." / "Become the version of yourself you keep putting off."*

### Authority
Establishes credibility via expertise, credentials, or institutional backing.
- **Best for:** Health, wellness, finance. Skeptical audiences.
- **Trigger:** Social Proof/Credibility
- *"Dermatologist recommended." / "Backed by 12 peer-reviewed studies."*

### Belief
Opens with the brand's point of view, mission, or values.
- **Best for:** Brand-building, mission-driven products, values-aligned audiences.
- **Trigger:** Aspiration/Desire, Contrarian
- *"We believe skincare should be simple."*

### Bold Claim
Makes an outsized, extreme, or superlative promise.
- **Best for:** Competitive categories where differentiation is hard.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Urgency/Stakes
- *"The world's best..." / "Nothing else comes close."*

### Call To Action First
Opens with an explicit action instruction. Skips the buildup.
- **Best for:** Most-Aware audiences. Retargeting. Sales and offers.
- **Trigger:** Urgency/Stakes
- *"Shop now." / "Try it free for 14 days."*

### Challenge
Competitive framing that invites the viewer to test or prove something.
- **Best for:** Achievement-oriented audiences. Fitness, gaming, performance.
- **Trigger:** Identity Call-Out, Pattern Interrupt
- *"I bet you can not finish this." / "Take the 7-day challenge."*

### Confession
A candid admission that builds credibility through vulnerability.
- **Best for:** Rebuilding trust, countering skepticism, standing out in polished categories.
- **Trigger:** Social Proof/Credibility, Pattern Interrupt
- *"I was wrong about sunscreen." / "We messed up. Here is what we learned."*

### Contrast
Juxtaposes two things to highlight a mismatch or clear superiority.
- **Best for:** Price-sensitive audiences, upgrade messaging, competitive conquesting.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Pain Agitation
- *"Do not put $5 gas in a $50,000 car."*

### Contrarian
Deliberately goes against conventional wisdom.
- **Best for:** Educated, skeptical audiences. Categories full of conventional advice.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Myth-Busting
- *"I am a vet who does not take her dog to the vet."*

### Curiosity
Creates an open loop the viewer needs to close.
- **Best for:** Top of funnel, content-led ads, audiences not yet emotionally invested.
- **Trigger:** Curiosity Gap
- *"Water does not hydrate you." / "Wallpaper is not for walls."*

### Demographic Callout
Names a specific audience segment directly.
- **Best for:** Niche products, segmented audiences, precision over reach.
- **Trigger:** Identity Call-Out
- *"For runners with bad knees." / "Attention new parents."*

### Direct Address
Speaks directly and personally to the viewer.
- **Best for:** Any awareness stage. Especially effective in social video.
- **Trigger:** Identity Call-Out, Pattern Interrupt
- *"Hey you..." / "If you are seeing this..." / "Stop scrolling."*

### Directive
An imperative that instructs the viewer to change a behavior or mindset.
- **Best for:** Problem-Aware and Solution-Aware. Health, finance, self-improvement.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Pain Agitation
- *"Stop wasting money on tools you do not use." / "Do not settle for generic."*

### Exclusivity
Signals that access is selective or limited.
- **Best for:** Premium products, high-ticket offers, status-motivated audiences.
- **Trigger:** Urgency/Stakes, Identity Call-Out
- *"By invite only." / "For serious operators only."*

### Explainer
Explains the reason behind something using "why" framing.
- **Best for:** Problem-Aware audiences who do not understand root cause. Technical products.
- **Trigger:** Curiosity Gap, Myth-Busting
- *"Why your towels stay musty." / "Why your pipeline keeps stalling."*

### FOMO
Creates anxiety about missing a trend or social moment.
- **Best for:** Product-Aware. Viral products. Categories where peer adoption matters.
- **Trigger:** Urgency/Stakes, Social Proof
- *"Everyone is switching to this..." / "Do not be the last to try it."*

### How To
An instructional promise that teaches the viewer how to accomplish something.
- **Best for:** Problem-Aware and Solution-Aware audiences actively looking for solutions.
- **Trigger:** Curiosity Gap, Pain Agitation
- *"How to fix your pipeline in one afternoon." / "How to meal prep in 10 minutes."*

### If Then
Qualifies the viewer with a condition, then delivers a promise.
- **Best for:** Segmented audiences with a clear condition. Strong for direct response.
- **Trigger:** Identity Call-Out, Pain Agitation
- *"If you sit at a desk all day, do this." / "If your team uses Slack, try this."*

### Listicle
Numbered or list-based framing.
- **Best for:** Content-heavy campaigns, educational top-of-funnel, scannable formats.
- **Trigger:** Curiosity Gap, Social Proof
- *"5 steps to better code reviews." / "Top 10 tools for marketers."*

### Myth Busting
Directly debunks a widely held misconception.
- **Best for:** Sophisticated audiences. Categories where misinformation is common.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Myth-Busting
- *"SPF 100 does not block 100%." / "More leads does not mean more revenue."*

### Offer Only
Uses a discount or incentive as the sole hook.
- **Best for:** Most-Aware audiences. Sales events. Price-motivated segments.
- **Trigger:** Urgency/Stakes
- *"$500 off." / "Free for 30 days." / "Save 40% this week."*

### Price Anchor
Frames cost against a familiar benchmark.
- **Best for:** Mid-to-high ticket. Price-sensitive audiences.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Pain Agitation
- *"Less than your daily coffee." / "Cheaper than one bad hire."*

### Question
Opens with a posed problem or curiosity gap in question form.
- **Best for:** All awareness stages. Especially effective when mirroring internal monologue.
- **Trigger:** Curiosity Gap, Pain Agitation
- *"Struggling with X?" / "Did you know...?" / "What if you could...?"*

### Reasons Why
Opens with a number + "reasons why" phrasing.
- **Best for:** Solution-Aware and Product-Aware audiences needing logical justification.
- **Trigger:** Social Proof, Curiosity Gap
- *"3 reasons why teams are switching to this." / "7 reasons to stop building in-house."*

### Relatability
Anchors in a shared everyday scenario.
- **Best for:** Unaware and Problem-Aware. Lifestyle and consumer products. Broad reach.
- **Trigger:** Pain Agitation, Identity Call-Out
- *"When your coffee hits at 3pm..." / "When the Slack notification never stops..."*

### Reverse Psychology
Tells the viewer not to act to trigger reactance.
- **Best for:** Ad-fatigued audiences. Contrarian personas. Skeptical viewers.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Curiosity Gap
- *"Do not click this." / "This is not for you." / "Seriously, skip this one."*

### Risk Reversal
Reduces perceived risk with guarantees or safety nets.
- **Best for:** Product-Aware with hesitation. High-ticket or new-to-market products.
- **Trigger:** Social Proof, Urgency/Stakes
- *"30-day money-back guarantee." / "Cancel anytime. No questions asked."*

### Shocking Statement
Leads with a surprising or counter-intuitive claim.
- **Best for:** Any awareness stage. Stopping scroll on saturated feeds.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Myth-Busting
- *"You have been doing this wrong your whole career."*

### Social Proof
Leverages reviews, testimonials, or popularity signals.
- **Best for:** Product-Aware with hesitation. Any category where trust is a barrier.
- **Trigger:** Social Proof/Credibility
- *"10,000 5-star reviews." / "Used by 300 teams." / "Most-loved on G2."*

### Statistic
Uses quantified evidence to establish credibility.
- **Best for:** Skeptical, analytical audiences. B2B, health, finance.
- **Trigger:** Social Proof, Pattern Interrupt
- *"73% of teams report..." / "9 out of 10 users..."*

### Storytelling
Drops mid-moment into a personal or brand story.
- **Best for:** Any awareness stage. Emotional products or complex transformations.
- **Trigger:** Pain Agitation, Curiosity Gap, Social Proof
- *"Three years ago our pipeline was a spreadsheet..."*

### Urgency
Creates time or supply pressure.
- **Best for:** Most-Aware. Sales periods. Situations where inaction has a real cost.
- **Trigger:** Urgency/Stakes
- *"Ends tonight." / "Only 50 seats left." / "Prices go up Monday."*

### Warning
Issues a sincere caution that halts default behavior.
- **Best for:** Problem-Aware audiences about to make a mistake.
- **Trigger:** Pattern Interrupt, Pain Agitation
- *"Do not buy another tool until you see this." / "Stop before you sign that contract."*
