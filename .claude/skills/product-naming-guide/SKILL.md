# Product Naming Guide

## Naming Patterns

| Pattern | How It Works | Examples | Best For |
|---------|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Descriptive** | Says what it does | Salesforce, YouTube | Clarity, SEO |
| **Invented** | Made-up word | Spotify, Figma | Distinctiveness, trademark |
| **Metaphorical** | Borrows from another domain | Amazon, Slack, Snowflake | Memorability |
| **Compound** | Two words merged | WordPress, Airbnb | Descriptive + memorable |
| **Acronym** | Shortened form | AWS, VSCode | Efficiency (if long form is known) |

## Process

**Round 1 — Volume (20+):** Generate across multiple patterns. Don't judge yet.
**Round 2 — Filter (8-10):** Remove names that are hard to spell after hearing once, hard to pronounce, sound like an existing brand, have negative connotations in major languages, or are longer than 3 syllables.
**Round 3 — Score top candidates (1-5 each):**

| Criterion | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Memorable** | Recall after hearing once? |
| **Searchable** | Unique search footprint? |
| **Extendable** | Can you build sub-brands around it? |
| **Ownable** | .com available? Social handles? |
| **Fits positioning** | Reinforces or fights the positioning? |

**Round 4 — Validate top 3:** Domain check, trademark search (USPTO/EUIPO), social handles, say-it-out-loud test, phone test.

## Anti-Patterns
- Generic + AI suffix ("[Thing]AI")
- Forced misspelling that needs explanation
- Too clever (needs a footnote for the pun)
- Unpronounceable consonant clusters
- Category name as brand name
- Inside jokes that only the founding team gets
