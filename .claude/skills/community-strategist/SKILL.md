# Community Strategist

Design communities that people actually participate in. Most communities fail because they are broadcast channels disguised as communities.

## Writes
- `outputs/community-strategy-[name].md`

## Step 1: Define the Community

1. **Who is it for?** Specific person, not "our customers"
2. **Why would they join?** What value do they get that they cannot get elsewhere?
3. **Why would they stay?** First-visit value is different from ongoing value
4. **What does success look like?** Active discussions, user-generated content, peer support, feedback loop

## Step 2: Platform Selection

| Platform | Best For | Watch Out For |
|---|---|---|
| Discord | Real-time chat, dev/creator communities, younger audiences | Can feel chaotic without structure, hard to search |
| Slack | Professional communities, B2B, structured discussions | Gets noisy, free tier limits history |
| Circle | Branded community, courses, structured content | Paid, requires critical mass to feel alive |
| GitHub Discussions | Open source, developer tools | Only works if your audience lives on GitHub |
| LinkedIn Groups | Professional networking, B2B content | Low engagement, algorithm-dependent |
| Reddit | Public discussion, SEO value, large-scale communities | Moderation-intensive, hostile to self-promotion |

## Step 3: Structure

**Channel/space design:**
- Keep channels under 10 at launch. Add as demand emerges.
- Every channel needs a clear purpose stated in the description
- Have one "general" space for off-topic conversation
- Create a "wins" or "show and tell" channel (positive energy)
- Have a "help" channel with clear guidelines for asking questions

**Roles:**
- New member (limited permissions first 24-48 hours)
- Member (full access)
- Contributor (recognized active members)
- Moderator (community-selected, not just staff)
- Admin (team members)

## Step 4: Engagement Programs

**Week 1 — New member onboarding:**
- Welcome message within 24 hours (personal, not automated if possible)
- "Introduce yourself" prompt with specific questions
- Point them to the most active discussion

**Ongoing:**
- Weekly discussion prompts or themed days
- Highlight member contributions publicly
- Ask for input on product decisions (make them feel heard)
- Host periodic events (AMAs, workshops, challenges)

## Step 5: Growth

**Organic:**
- Make membership the CTA in content (blog, newsletter, social)
- Member referrals (give existing members a reason to invite others)
- SEO-visible content from community discussions (if platform allows)

**Activation metrics:**
- Day 1: Did they post or react?
- Week 1: Did they return?
- Month 1: Did they help someone else?

A community of 50 active members is worth more than 5,000 silent ones.
