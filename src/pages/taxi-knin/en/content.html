Your Long-Distance Taxi in Knin

Knin is the historic inland town at the foot of its medieval fortress, a regional hub with its own railway station on the Zagreb to Split line. It is the furthest point I serve, so a Knin transfer is a proper intercity drive: down to Šibenik and the coast, or all the way to Split or Zadar airport. Because of the distance I always agree the fixed price and the pickup time up front, whether I am meeting your train at the station or collecting you from home.

How My Knin Taxi Works

Šibenik-Based, Booked Ahead for Knin

To be clear, I am a private taxi based in the Šibenik and Skradin area, not a rank cab parked in Knin. I come to you for pre-arranged transfers, so booking ahead is how it works. I am not the driver to flag down for a two-minute hop around Knin.

What I do run from Knin are the trips worth booking a car for: the ride into Šibenik or Skradin, airport transfers to Split and Zadar, and longer journeys across Croatia. These private transfers typically start from around €95 and go up with the distance.

I also take Knin guests to Krka National Park, either the Lozovac road entrance or the Skradin boat entrance, one way or as a full round-trip day out while I wait. Airport transfers are the rides I am asked for most, so tell me your flight and I will confirm a fixed price.

Do you meet trains at Knin station?

Yes, and agreed up front given the distance. Knin to Šibenik is €95, Split Airport €110 and Zadar Airport €140 per car for up to 4 passengers.

Yes. I can wait or come back for you the same day. Tell me the timings and I will confirm a fixed price for the round trip.
